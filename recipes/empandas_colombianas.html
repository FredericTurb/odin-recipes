Empanadas Colombianas

Description of the dish:

Empanadas colombianas are a popular traditional snack or street food in Colombia.
They are pastry pockets that are typically filled with a variety of ingredients and then fried or baked.

Here is the receipe for the beef and potatoes version, the most common one.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:
  1. 2 cups corn flour (masarepa).
  2. 1 cup warm water.
  3. 1/2 teaspoon salt.
  4. 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional, for added richness).
For the Filling:
  1. 1 cup ground beef.
  2. 1 cup potatoes, peeled and diced into small cubes.
  3. 1 small onion, finely chopped.
  4. 1 garlic clove minced.
  5. 1/2 teaspoon cumin.
  6. 1/2 teaspoon paprika (optional).
  7. Salt and pepper to taste.
  8. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for cooking).
  9. 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (optional).
For Frying:
  1. Vegetable oil (for frying).
Optional Ingredients:
  1. Aji sauce for serving (a Colombian spicy sauce made from tomatoes, onions, and peppers).
  2. Cheese (some variations include cheese in the filling).

Preparation:

  • Cook the Potatoes: In a pot, boil the diced potatoes in salted water until tender (about 10-15 minutes). Drain and set aside.
  • Cook the Beef: Season with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook until the beef is browned.
  • Combine: Add the cooked potatoes to the beef mixture and mix well. Adjust seasoning if necessary. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  • Prepare the Dough: Knead the dough briefly until smooth. Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball) and cover with a damp cloth to prevent drying.
  • Assemble the Empanadas: Roll, fill and seal.
  • Fry the Empanadas: Fry the empanadas in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Cook until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes on each side.

Serve !
